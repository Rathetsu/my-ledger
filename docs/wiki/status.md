**P8 (wishlist).** Wishlist items are a per-user table (`wishlist_items`, integer minor-unit `cost_minor`, the shared `currency` enum, a `wishlist_status` enum of planned/purchased) with full CRUD via the now-standard split: zod schemas in `lib/actions/schemas.ts`, DB logic in a plain `userId`-taking `lib/wishlist.ts`, and thin `'use server'` wrappers in `lib/actions/wishlist.ts` that export only async functions. Buying an item posts a single source-linked `purchase` transaction (stored negative, in the item's currency) and flips the item to purchased atomically in one `dbPool.transaction`; un-purchase reverses it. Both money-writes uphold the archived-account write-freeze: purchase refuses an archived target account (checked on the loaded row inside the tx), and un-purchase refuses to delete a transaction out of an account that has since been archived (the `unconfirmOccurrence` analog). A wishlist item is not an active definition targeting an account, so wishlist adds no `archiveBlockers` clause, which is why that per-write guard is the sole defense; both directions are covered by DB-backed tests. The pure planner engine gained wishlist funding: each month `buildPlan` funds items from `unallocatedMinor` (the deadline-slack + post-debt surplus it already computed), target-dated items first at the level amount that makes them affordable by their target date, then the rest by priority, filling `MonthPlan.wishlistFunding` and `PlanResult.wishlistAffordablePeriod` without changing the reported `unallocatedMinor` or the debt allocation above it.
